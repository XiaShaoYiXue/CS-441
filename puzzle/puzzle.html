Brushstroke of Silence

By Xiaonan Li and Molly Han


We were told to bend like reeds,
To weave love softly, to serve others' needs.
They call us fragile, soft as art,
Yet bar our hands from playing a part.

I've been shattered and pieced back anew,
Like a puzzle discarded, missing a few.
Men hold the throne, they rule and decide,
Yet in my soul, it's paint that guides.


On average, museums collect 12 artworks per male artist compared to 8 per female artist.
