Brushstrokes of Silence

We were told to bend like reeds,

To weave love softly, to serve others' needs.

They call us fragile, soft as art,

Yet bar our hands from playing a part.

I've been shattered and pieced back anew,

Like a puzzle discarded, missing a few.

Men hold the throne, they rule and decide,

Yet in my soul, it's paint that guides.

I long to draw what my heart perceives,

The feelings, the echoes my soul believes.

Yet roles and burdens steal time away,

My art must wear a man's name to stay.

A whisper lost in history's air,

A canvas left in disrepair.

How many hands were brushed aside?

How many dreams were cast to hide?

Their works hang high, their visions bright,

Collected fast, displayed in light.

The halls stretch wide with frames of gold,

Yet women's names are dim and cold.

Men paint with little, yet still survive,

Their art takes shape, their dreams alive.

A brush, a board, a coin a day,

No wealth required to leave a trace.

But she, with talent, locked outside,

Watches colors drain and hide.

Painting small, her work stays unseen,

No space reserved where men have been.

Her strokes are vast, her canvas grand,

She must make space, she must expand.

Not from choice, but from demand—

A fight for space, to take a stand.

Her hands once bound, her choices slim,

No room to shape the art within.

But voices rise, our colors spread,

We break the chains, paint bold instead.

The dots of color help me live,

With art, my soul will always give.

Men call me great—among women's best,

But I know I stand with all the rest.
